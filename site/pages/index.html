When does a costly trait become worth keeping?

active_monkey is a tiny artificial-life lab. It uses small artificial ecologies to test when costly sensing, passive memory, active information-gathering, and later communication become locally adaptive under pressure.

The framing

Ecology before capability.

Most AI starts with a task and asks how capable the model can get. This lab starts with pressure: a tiny system in a world with finite resources, sensory limits, competition, inheritance, and consequences.

world

The world has consequence

A variable matters only if it changes whether the creature can continue, reproduce, or act better.

representation

Information has to earn its place

The central question is when a world variable becomes worth sensing, remembering, actively sampling, compressing, or communicating from the creature's side.

discipline

Emergence gets tested

A result counts only when direct reward, trivial baselines, seed accidents, and observer-only explanations have been made hard to confuse with the claim.

The research spine

Ecology → local gradient → active sensing.

The public story is not experiment 1 versus experiment 3. It is a self-lab with a spine: build minimal conditions where costly information-bearing traits have to earn their place.

01

Ecological pressure

Energy, depletion, temperature, competition, and lineage pressure make some information useful and most information ignorable.

02

Costly sensing

A world variable becomes accessible. The test is whether the ecology makes the sensor useful without rewarding the sensor directly.

03

Local-gradient wall

Gifted sensors and passive memory can be useful, while small costed steps still fail to beat the resident.

04

Active sensing

The next test is whether a creature can pay to probe, sample, or scan for better information before acting.

05

Communication and world models

Signals and learned models remain future motivation; they must change belief or action under pressure without direct reward leakage.

The lab right now

A clean self-lab, not a demo reel.

The project is a public notebook for one research direction: how small artificial systems become more creature-like under pressure, and which explanations survive attempts to break them.

current

Phase 4 active sensing

Test whether a costly probe or sampling action can improve future action selection enough to win locally.

current evidence

Passive memory wall

Hidden-state memory and belief-persistence variants have not yet produced a robust local adaptive gradient.

later

Active inference

Couple action and perception more deeply only after the active-sensing bridge survives its falsifiers.
