================================================================================================
Exp 259 — DISCRETE RED QUEEN on the patch-mosaic substrate. CANONICAL SUMMARY.
================================================================================================

QUESTION
--------
Does co-evolution of the predator (single causal bit = freeze_predator_trait: True=static,
False=co-evolving) change the evolutionary outcome of prey escape-speed on the discrete
patch-mosaic substrate (BOTH regime: refuge+migration+asynchrony)?

FALSIFIER
---------
If the static and co-evolving arms are indistinguishable — predator attack does not adapt,
prey escape outcome unchanged — co-evolution does not engage here.

SETUP
-----
experiments/exp259_patch_red_queen.py.
BOTH regime (n_patches=8 ring, refuge access_fraction=0.30/refuge_frac=0.25,
migration_rate=0.05, async rotating amp=0.4, collapse-prone within-patch
attack_a=0.05/K_pred=40/hmax=0.05), enable_trait_evolution.
Trait-evolution extension commit 3736adf (byte-identical OFF; ring golden T10 unchanged).
Sanity seeds 300-302 confirmed coexistence survives evolving traits.
VERDICT on FRESH seeds 400-409.

Part A: invasion-from-rarity — rare breed-true mutant (escape=1.4) into resident
        (escape=1.0) at equilibrium; static vs co-evolving predator.
Part B: open-ended arms race — prey escape mutable; predator static vs co-evolving;
        trait means at checkpoints 500/1000/2000/3000, horizon=3000.

RESULT
------
Part A (invasion-from-rarity):
  static predator:   6/10 seeds invaded (mutant fixed 6/10)
  co-evolving pred:  0/10 seeds invaded (adapting predator neutralizes escape advantage)

Part B (open-ended arms race):
  arm         esc@500  esc@1k  esc@2k  esc@3k  atk@3k  extinct@end
  static       1.39    2.02    2.69    2.98    1.00    0/10
  co-evolve    1.14    0.92    0.77    0.68    1.59    0/10

  static:   prey escape escalates to 2.98; predator attack FROZEN at 1.00.
  co-evolve: predator attack ESCALATES 1.0 -> 1.59; prey escape COLLAPSES
             (monotone decline 1.14->0.92->0.77->0.68 — prey abandon a now-futile
             costly trait). All arms coexist to horizon (0/10 extinct).

VERDICT: POSITIVE-SINGLE
------------------------
Co-evolution (single causal bit) demonstrably changes the outcome:

  - Flips invasion:  static 6/10 invaded -> co-evolve 0/10 (adapting predator neutralizes
                     escape advantage)
  - Drives predator-attack escalation: 1.0 -> 1.59 under co-evolution
  - Prey-escape suppression: 2.98 (static) -> 0.68 (co-evolve) = predator-DOMINANCE

The dynamic is ASYMMETRIC PREDATOR-DOMINANCE (attack rises + plateaus ~1.6; prey escape
suppressed below baseline), NOT a sustained mutual arms race.

SUBSTRATE-DEPENDENCE: the co-evolutionary outcome is substrate-dependent.
  - Well-mixed Exp 256:           co-evolution -> prey CO-ESCALATION (esc up to 3.88)
  - Discrete patch (this exp):    co-evolution -> PREDATOR-DOMINANCE (esc collapses to 0.68)

Co-evolution matters on both substrates; its direction depends on the substrate.

HONEST CAVEATS
--------------
1. Trait expressibility is BY-CONSTRUCTION (capture = f(escape, attack)); the EMERGENT
   results are: invasion-flip, predator-attack escalation, prey-escape suppression,
   and the substrate-dependence vs well-mixed.
2. The outcome is predator-DOMINANCE, NOT a classic sustained mutual Red Queen escalation.
3. Within-patch dynamics are mean-field; collapse-prone regime calibrated;
   coexistence scaffolded by the BOTH (refuge+migration) regime.
4. Part A static invasion = 6/10 (metapopulation stochasticity), vs 0/10 co-evolve
   (clean contrast).

Self-grade: POSITIVE-SINGLE (scoped positive; discrete Red Queen engages; outcome is
predator-dominance, substrate-dependent vs well-mixed; expressibility by-construction;
NOT breakthrough-level).

Rung 4 of the patch-mosaic-red-queen ladder complete.
