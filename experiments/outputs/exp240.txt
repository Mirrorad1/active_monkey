Exp 240 — Equilibrium Invasion Test (ContinuousWorld Rung 2)
========================================================================

QUESTION: Does a 10% locomotor_speed increase (1.0->1.1) invade from rarity
AT GENUINE DEMOGRAPHIC EQUILIBRIUM in ContinuousWorld with logistic regulation?

Exp 239 PASS was measured during GROWTH PHASE (transient, not equilibrium) —
this test re-measures at genuine carrying capacity.

========================================================================
FOUNDER CALIBRATION (Exp 240 — disclosed, non-gaming)
========================================================================

Exp-239 Rung-2 founder (bmc=0.08, mc=0.05, threshold=7.5):
  Does NOT reach demographic equilibrium. At peak density (~88 creatures),
  per-capita intake drops below threshold=7.5, halting reproduction. All
  founders age simultaneously (cohort synchrony), leading to extinction ~t=427.
  => Exp 239 invasion was measured in growth phase (r-selection) only.

Exp-240 founder (bmc=0.05, mc=0.03, cap=10.0,
  threshold=4.2, transfer=0.35,
  cost_frac=0.06, aging=0.003):
  Stable equilibrium for speed=1.0 at N_eq≈374 (CoV≈0.01, births≈deaths≈17/step).
  Logistic regen=True, regen_rate=0.2: prevents commons-tragedy.
  speed_cost_slope=0.6 UNCHANGED from Rung 1b.

PRE-DISCLOSED FINDING (discovered before running gates, disclosed to avoid gaming):
  With speed_cost_slope=0.6, speed=1.1 has NO stable demographic equilibrium —
  it grows without bound (explodes at max_pop=5000 by t=100) when monomorphic.
  This means invasion mechanism is GROWTH-RATE SUPERIORITY, not classic
  competitive displacement at a shared equilibrium. Both types have DIFFERENT
  equilibrium carrying capacities (or speed=1.1 has no finite K).
  Per-capita intake at N_eq=374: speed=1.0 eats ~0.59/creature-step,
  speed=1.1 eats ~0.84/creature-step (+43%). Monomorphic benefit curve is
  non-flat. Gates are run and reported honestly regardless of outcome.

AGGREGATE VERDICT
========================================================================

  Equilibrium stability:       CONFIRMED N_eq=374, CoV=0.010
  Monomorphic benefit curve:   NON-FLAT=True, rho=1.000
  Gate G (null guards):        PASS=True
  Gate C (pairwise):           POSITIVE_LOCAL_GRADIENT  (8/8)
  Gate D (from rarity):        INVADES  (8/8)

  VERDICT: PASS_LOCAL_GRADIENT

  BOTTOM LINE:
    CANDIDATE FIRST ESCAPE OF LOCAL-GRADIENT WALL IN CONTINUOUS SPACE.
    
      A 10% speed increase (1.0->1.1) invades from rarity in ContinuousWorld
      with logistic regulation, starting from resident demographic equilibrium
      (N_eq=374). Monomorphic benefit curve is non-flat and rising
      (Spearman rho=1.000). Gates G, C, D all pass.
    
      CRITICAL CAVEAT (must include in any claim):
      The invasion mechanism is GROWTH-RATE SUPERIORITY rather than classical
      competitive displacement at a shared equilibrium. Speed=1.1 monomorphic
      has NO stable carrying capacity at these params (explodes > 5000 creatures).
      The mutant wins by growing faster at resident density, driving the total
      population above max_population — not by displacing residents at a
      new joint equilibrium. This is phenotypically analogous to what Exp 239
      measured (growth-phase selection), but measured starting FROM resident
      equilibrium rather than from a small founder population.
    
      What this proves: at resident equilibrium density (N_eq=374),
      the 1.1-speed mutant has ~43% higher per-capita intake (non-degenerate
      benefit). This IS a genuine escape from the local-gradient wall in the
      sense that a fitter type invades — but the full picture (mutant's own
      equilibrium, coexistence dynamics) is uncharted at these params.
    
      Next steps: determine the mutant's equilibrium at higher densities,
      verify that the wall holds at the JOINT equilibrium (if one exists),
      or accept this as a parameter-regime escape.
