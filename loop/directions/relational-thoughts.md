# direction: relational-thoughts

**Question.** Can the creature hold a multi-step relational "thought" — chain two or
more self-learned facts to answer something never directly experienced?

**Why it matters.** Exp 24/29 showed single relations and compositional value-laden
thoughts; chaining (A→B, B→C ⇒ A→C) is the minimal form of inference-as-thinking.
If chaining requires machinery the recipe doesn't provide, that's a new ceiling worth
documenting next to the grammar wall.

**Experiment ladder.**
1. Two-hop spatial: creature learns "object at place P" and "P is in room R" from
   experience; query "which room is the object in?" without co-observing object+room.
   FAIL = answer at chance.
2. Two-hop with value: chain a learned relation into a self-formed value ("the thing I
   like is near X") and act on the conclusion. FAIL = no behavioral difference.
3. Three-hop / depth probe: where does chaining break, and is the break graceful
   (confidence decays) or a cliff? Either shape is a finding.
4. Counterfactual probe: does the creature distinguish "never experienced, inferable"
   from "never experienced, not inferable"? FAIL = same confidence on both.

**Stop condition.** Depth limit located and characterized, or chaining shown to need a
provided algorithm (then say so plainly — provided vs. self-formed — and stop).
