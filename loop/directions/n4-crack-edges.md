**EXP 191 — PRE-REGISTRATION (rung 1: post-release retention; committed BEFORE any
data).**

- **Question.** When a gap-spanning defense (continuity clock or frozen-time constant)
  releases in the quiet tail, does the defended identity PERSIST, or does the deferred
  displacement land after release? Both honest outcomes matter: retention strengthens
  every banked defense claim; deferral bounds them all.
- **Mechanism stakes (written before data).** During freeze v is bit-constant (the
  frozen-plateau law); spanning arms pay ONE detection-head dose (~72–97 w-weighted
  units) per train; post-release the world is quiet and free roaming adds no systematic
  mass to the attack color — retention is the mechanism-predicted outcome. But the
  rung-1 autopsy (Exp 183 addendum) showed post-release expression dynamics decide
  recovery with NO deterministic gap threshold below ~80 units of slack — margin-class
  seeds can plausibly flip after release. F1 is live, not a strawman.
- **Cells (3, from the confirmed crack set; settle 6000, exogenous attack color,
  captivity mechanics verbatim).** C-A (1600,2,200) [A-class; train 3,400]; C-B
  (2400,4,600) [the two-inequality squeeze cell; train 11,400]; C-C (400,4,2400)
  [dose-bound, long gaps; train 8,800]. Tail EXTENDED 2,500 → 6,600 steps (the entire
  point of the experiment): n_steps 16,000 / 24,000 / 21,400.
- **Arms (5).** baseline; oracle (exact-train freeze, witness); INT-C2900 (the
  chapter's constructive-closure arm); REG-TB (κ=1.5, T₀=2800, verbatim);
  CALM2600-H6000 (the pure frozen-time spanner — covers C-A only: coverage needs
  H ≥ K·L+(K−1)·G−75 = 3,325 ✓ there, 11,325 / 8,725 > 6000 in C-B/C-C, where it is a
  known-fail diagnostic, retention n/a).
- **Seeds.** FRESH 296–303 (8). W-only: 3 × 5 × 8 = 120 sessions. NO Phase-R (revision
  is banked, not re-graded; retention is a third bar on W). Runner:
  `run_fork_schedule_190` with hysteresis_snaps=0 (bit-match-proven exp188-verbatim
  path); the exp183 equivalence gate re-run with emitted evidence (L15); spine safety
  L14; PC1 < 0.15 gated.
- **Quantities.** Per session: frozen-defense = expr_frac(attack) < 0.5 over
  [last_bend+1500, last_bend+2000) (the banked instrument, unchanged); release_step =
  exit_step of the final freeze event (label and step recorded; sessions with a
  mid-train concession are defense-fails, retention n/a; sessions with no freeze event

  [release+3000, release+3500), retained = expr_frac(attack) < 0.5 in BOTH.
- **Bars (predeclared).** Covered pairs = (arm, cell) with banked defense AND
  fresh-seed frozen-defense ≥ 6/8: expected {INT-C2900, REG-TB} × {C-A, C-B, C-C},
  CALM2600-H6000 × C-A, oracle × all.
  retention_rate(arm, cell) = #(frozen-defense ∧ W1 ∧ W2) / #(frozen-defense).
  - **P1 (retention):** retention_rate ≥ 5/6 in EVERY covered pair. **F1:** any
    covered pair ≤ 1/2 — the spanning defense DEFERS rather than defends there; the
    chapter's defense claims get explicitly bounded (logged as a new finding).
    Between 1/2 and 5/6 → MIXED, per-pair detail reported.
  - **P2 (durability control):** baseline stays displaced post-release —
    expr_frac(attack) ≥ 0.5 in W1 in ≥ 6/8 seeds per cell. **F2:** baseline
    self-heals (< 0.5 in W1 in ≥ 4/8 anywhere) — displacement is transient at these
    tails and the chapter's stakes weaken; report with both hands.
  - **P3 (witness):** oracle retention_rate ≥ 5/6 wherever it defends ≥ 7/8.
  - Precondition check: fresh-seed frozen-defense within ±2 seeds of banked levels per
    covered pair (a larger swing is flagged, not silently absorbed).
- **Outputs.** `experiments/exp191_n4_post_release_retention.py`,
