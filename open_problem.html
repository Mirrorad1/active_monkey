The open problem, precisely Why a free-energy agent won’t grow a concept (a “topic”, a “self”, an opinion) on its own — and exactly where the mathematics stops

Goal: a discrete active-inference agent that learns language and forms its own dispositions from scratch — never pre-trained on opinions. The experiments hit a wall whose shape is well-defined. This note states that wall in math.

We are asking the model to discover, unsupervised, a latent variable it was never given — a “topic”/concept \(z\) — purely by minimizing free energy. That is the crux, and it is genuinely hard.

The model

A hierarchical state-space (active-inference) model: a slow latent \(z\) (topic, near-identity dynamics so it persists), a fast latent \(s_t\) (character context), discrete emissions \(o_t\) (characters), with Dirichlet priors on the parameters \(A,B\):

$$p(o_{1:T}, s_{1:T}, z, A, B) \;=\; p(z)\,p(A)\,p(B)\prod_{t}\; \underbrace{p(o_t \mid s_t; A)}_{\text{emission}}\;\underbrace{p(s_t \mid s_{t-1}, z; B)}_{\text{topic-conditioned transition}}$$

We minimize variational free energy over a mean-field posterior \(q(z)\,q(s_{1:T})\,q(A)\,q(B)\):

$$F \;=\; \mathbb{E}_q\!\big[\ln q - \ln p\big] \;=\; \underbrace{-\,\mathbb{E}_q\!\big[\ln p(o\mid s,A)\big]}_{\text{inaccuracy (surprise)}} \;+\; \underbrace{\mathrm{KL}\!\big[q(z,s,A,B)\,\Vert\,p(z,s,A,B)\big]}_{\text{complexity}}.$$

the open problem in one sentence

This objective has a dominant fixed point at which the latent \(z\) is useless, and nothing in the dynamics pushes the system off it. Three exact reasons, plus a separate long-range sub-problem.

1A symmetric saddle — no gradient to break the symmetry

If \(B\) is symmetric in \(z\) (all topics identical at initialization), then \(q(z{=}k)=1/K\) is stationary and

$$\left.\frac{\partial F}{\partial(\text{topic differentiation})}\right|_{\text{symmetric}} = 0 .$$

There is literally zero first-order force driving the topics to specialize. Worse, the entropy term \(-\,\mathbb{E}_q[\ln q(z)]\) inside \(F\) actively smooths any small fluctuation back toward uniform. Escaping requires a second-order fluctuation to be amplified, and mean-field damps exactly those.

observed — Exp 12

sky is → "s gree"   grass is → "s gree"   topic belief stayed [0.5, 0.5] the entire run. The latent never differentiated.

2Non-identifiability — and complexity prefers collapse

The marginal likelihood \(p(o_{1:T}\mid A,B)\) can be explained two ways:

(a) one topic, with averaged transitions over the mixture.
(b) two topics, each specialized to its clause type.

These are near-degenerate in accuracy — the latent partition is non-identifiable without an inductive bias. And the complexity term \(\mathrm{KL}[q\,\Vert\,p]\) rewards the simpler, topic-agnostic model: fewer effective states, lower KL. Bayesian Occam (model reduction) actively pulls toward not using \(z\).

This is the same phenomenon as posterior collapse in VAEs: the latent goes unused because ignoring it costs ~nothing in accuracy and saves complexity.

3The EM chicken-and-egg (credit assignment)

To infer \(q(z)\) you need topic-differentiated \(B\); to learn differentiated \(B\) you need an informative \(q(z)\). Symmetric init gives no bootstrap, and variational EM only finds local optima. The “use the latent” basin and the “ignore the latent” basin are separated — and you start in the second one.

observed — Exp 13

Even with the topic teacher-forced during training, at test the inferred topic returned to [0.5,0.5] and binding failed — because the emission \(A\) is topic-independent, so the subject has no direct evidential pathway to move \(q(z)\); the only channel is transition-mismatch, which single-step inference does not accumulate.

The separate sub-problem: long-range precision (binding)

Even granting a topic, the dependency “sky … is blue” spans 5+ steps. The filtering posterior is Markov:

$$q(s_t) \;\propto\; p(o_t\mid s_t)\sum_{s_{t-1}} p(s_t\mid s_{t-1})\,q(s_{t-1}),$$

so it forgets: by “is”, every trace of “sky” has been squeezed through one belief (d-separation). Only a dedicated slow factor, held with high precision, survives — but learning that persistence-precision again presupposes the topic is already informative.

observed — Exp 9

"sky is " → "green" and "grass is " → "green": a fixed-order Markov model cannot bind across a span longer than its order, at any state count (Exp 4: more states \(K\) did not help — capacity ≠ memory).

What this is called, and why it’s open

It is unsupervised structure / representation learning: discovering which latent variables to posit (how many, at what timescale) from data alone, by free-energy minimization. Mathematically it is the conjunction of:

  • a non-convex objective with a dominant uninformative-latent basin;
  • non-identifiability of latents without inductive bias;
  • a complexity term that prefers collapsing the latent (Bayesian model reduction working against you);
  • long-range credit assignment under Markov forgetting.

There is no general, reliable algorithm that solves this by the free-energy principle alone, unsupervised. It is the same wall that makes from-scratch deep latent-variable learning hard everywhere.

What actually breaks the symmetry (the fixes)

Inductive biasWhat it does mathematically
KL annealing / β-warmupDown-weights the complexity term early so the latent is used before Occam prunes it.
Asymmetric init / perturbationSeeds a fluctuation large enough to be amplified rather than smoothed.
Structure learning + Bayesian model reductionGrows/prunes latents instead of fixing \(K\) — searches model space, not just parameters.
Precision / attention (learned precisions)Protects the slow factor’s belief from Markov forgetting → enables long-range holding.
Grounding signalMakes \(z\) identifiable: a cue tied to the latent gives the gradient a foothold.
observed — Exp 14 (the grounded path works)

An arbitrary cue acquired valence purely by co-occurring with the agent’s own low-free-energy states — P(next | …a)=3.04 bits vs P(next | …z)=4.79 bits. No labels. Where the symmetric-emergent path stalls, the grounded path crystallizes — exactly as the math predicts.

Why this is the crux of the moonshot

An “opinion” is a learned latent disposition. For one to emerge, never pre-trained, the system must perform exactly this unsupervised abstraction discovery — and the mathematics says it will not crystallize from symmetry; it needs something to condense around. That is not a dead end. It is a precise design constraint:

The path to emergent, non-pre-trained cognition is grounding + structure-learning inductive biases — not pure self-organization from nothing. Every result that worked (depth → words & Q→A, held-topic binding, self-grounded valence) came from giving the latent a foothold. Every result that stalled was the symmetric, foothold-free case.
