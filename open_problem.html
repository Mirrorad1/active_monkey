active_monkey · current research note

The open problem is creaturehood.

The current question is not whether a small system looks intelligent from the outside. It is whether ecological pressure can make sensing, persistent capability, belief, and communication useful from the system's own side.

Open problem: under what conditions does information become meaningful to a tiny artificial creature?

one sentence version

Build a small ecology where a world variable becomes worth sensing, worth maintaining, worth remembering, and eventually worth communicating, without directly rewarding the representation itself.

This page used to center an older framing around worldview growth and identity continuity. Those remain useful notes in the notebook, but the cleaner public framing is now more basic: study how creature-like agency begins before trying to bolt on large-scale intelligence.

Why this is not just another agent demo

A demo can make behavior look interesting. A lab has to say what would kill the explanation.

Third-person state

Position, resource field, population, lineage, births, deaths, trait distributions, and experiment outcomes.

First-person state

Local observations, internal energy, memory, prediction, uncertainty, belief update, and action.

The bridge between those two views is the core research direction. The outside observer can see the full world. The creature cannot. If the creature starts carrying internal estimates that improve action, then belief-like state becomes something we can measure.

The research spine

StageQuestionFailure mode to avoid
EcologyDoes the world create real pressure: resource limits, depletion, temperature, competition, or lineage effects?A toy world where every behavior works because nothing matters.
SensorDoes a world variable become useful to observe?Rewarding the sensor directly and calling that emergence.
OrganDoes the costly capability persist across generations because it improves action-relevant outcomes?A one-off mutation that appears but is not maintained.
BeliefDoes the creature carry internal estimates of hidden state that change action?Confusing observer annotations with the creature's own state.
CommunicationDoes one creature's signal change another creature's belief or action?Counting emitted symbols without showing that they matter.
World modelCan learned compression predict future trajectories or guide action without replacing the ecology as ground truth?Training on simulator artifacts and mistaking overfit for intelligence.

Current standard of evidence

A claim should name the causal variable and the thing that would falsify it. For sensor-to-organ work, that means the capability has a cost, the founder baseline is clear, the null arms are honest, and the experiment does not smuggle the answer into the reward.

Keep

Fixed seeds, raw outputs, null results, predeclared pass/fail bars, and a clear note about what was ruled out.

Do not count

Pretty curves, direct capability rewards, post-hoc thresholds, or success that disappears under trivial baselines.

Where deep active inference fits

Deep active inference is not the first move. It becomes useful when explicit belief variables stop scaling.

For now, the project should keep the substrate legible: ecology first, then sensing, then persistent capability, then belief reconstruction. Learned latent models can enter later as compression for action-relevant state, not as a shortcut around the hard part.

honest bottom line

active_monkey is not claiming AGI, consciousness, or language emergence. The defensible claim is narrower: tiny ecologies can be used to study how sensing, belief-like state, and world models become useful under pressure.
