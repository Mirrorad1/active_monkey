When does a costed sense become an organ?

A seven-experiment chapter (Exp 199–207) that turned the loose goal "make the heat-sensor win" into one sharp, falsifiable question — and answered it cleanly. The answer is no, and the reason is precise.

The result in one line: a costed sense does not evolve into a functional organ at this toy scale, because the local selection gradient at the resident is never positive — even when a precise population is genuinely fitter in bulk, demographically affordable, and survives.

\[ \boxed{\;\; g(h_{\text{res}}) \;=\; \frac{d\,\mathbb{E}[\,w \mid h\,]}{dh}\Bigg|_{h=h_{\text{res}}} \;\le\; 0 \quad\text{across all seven levers} \;\;} \]
why this is a real finding, not a failure

Each of the seven attempts had a predeclared falsifier, fixed seeds, honest null arms, and a blinded verifier; none smuggled the answer into the reward. A wall that survives that scrutiny tells you where the barrier actually is — here, the marginal step, not the headline benefit.

The reframe

Four earlier walls (Exp 199–202) showed a costed thermosense organ — a noisy heat reading the policy can act on, at a metabolic upkeep cost — never becomes functional, and under competition is actively suppressed. A gifted strong sensor reproduces far more than a no-sensor creature, so the installed benefit is real; evolution still does not climb. So the question stopped being "make it win" and became the reusable one:

the question

What environmental, informational, and evolutionary conditions are required for a costed sense-like trait to become increasingly selected over generations — to become organ-like rather than merely useful when gifted? Phrased so the answer transfers from heat-sensing to sight, hearing, localization, and communication.

The one distinction that decides everything

Write a creature's realized reproductive fitness as a benefit earned through its actions, minus the upkeep of the machinery it carries:

\[ w_i \;=\; \underbrace{B\!\left(a_i,\, z,\, h_i,\, \theta_i,\, \rho\right)}_{\text{benefit, via the chosen action }a_i} \;-\; \underbrace{C_h(h_i)}_{\text{sensor upkeep}} \;-\; \underbrace{C_\theta(\theta_i)}_{\text{controller upkeep}} \]
intuition

h is sensor precision, θ the controller's ability to use it, z the hidden world state, ρ the ecological state (density, depletion, crowding). Crucially the sensor only changes fitness by changing which action the creature takes — never by a term written directly on h. That "no reward on the representation" rule is what makes any positive result honest.

A population's mean sensor can only rise when the local selection gradient is positive near the current (resident) value — i.e. when a small heritable step already pays for itself:

\[ \text{a trait climbs} \iff \frac{dB}{dh}\bigg|_{h_{\text{res}}} \;>\; \frac{dC}{dh}\bigg|_{h_{\text{res}}} \;+\; \underbrace{\big(\text{drift} + \text{noise} + \text{erosion}\big)}_{\ge\,0} \]

Every wall in this chapter is the same trap: the installed benefit is huge while the marginal benefit at the resident is not:

\[ \underbrace{B(0.60) - B(0.00) \;\gg\; 0}_{\textbf{the gift is real}} \qquad\text{yet}\qquad \underbrace{B'(h_{\text{res}}) - C'(h_{\text{res}}) \;\le\; 0}_{\textbf{the small step doesn't pay}} \]
intuition

A forced strong sensor out-reproduces a blind one ~4× (Exp 200) — but evolution can only take small steps, and the first small step uphill costs more upkeep than it returns. A crude sensor already grabs the easy part of the benefit; precision's extra payoff is small and stochastic relative to its linear cost. The endpoint advantage tells you almost nothing about evolvability; only the sign of the slope at the resident does. This is the chapter's load-bearing lesson (logged as L22).

The meta-principle the whole sub-arc tested: a sense is evolvable when it exposes private actionable information whose marginal value stays positive across small heritable improvements. The instrument ecology/sense_axis.py measures exactly that slope — three density-robust estimators (monomorphic carrying capacity \(N^\*(h)\), the head-to-head pairwise selection coefficient \(s\), and density-independent intrinsic growth) — instead of the misleading endpoint gift.

The three shapes of failure

The walls are not all the same; they map the barrier from different sides.

1 · Saturation & concavity (199–202)

A crude sensor captures the easy part of any saturating benefit, so precision's marginal payoff is too small to out-breed its cost. "No Goldilocks gradient": the curve flattens exactly where a small step would have to pay.

2 · Frequency-dependence — and a private niche (206)

Make a class worth less when crowded, so precision pays more as rivals pile in (the hoped-for Red-Queen sign). The kept intake is discounted by a rival count — never by h:

\[ \text{kept}_i \;=\; \frac{\text{eaten}_i}{1 + \kappa\,N_{c(i)}}, \qquad \underbrace{\frac{\partial^2 B}{\partial h_i\,\partial \bar h_{-i}} > 0}_{\text{Red-Queen, hoped}} \]
intuition

\(N_{c}\) is the number of rivals on the cell's true class, \(\kappa\) the crowding strength; the class rotates over time so a static learned map can't memorize it (the load-bearing anti-cheat fix). The hope: a precise reader dodges to the under-crowded class and pays less. The result: it works for a crude reader too, so precision is never required — h decays at a healthy population. I(h;niche) ≈ 0 because h never varied.

3 · The controller, and the 2-D valley that wasn't (207)

Maybe a sharper sensor is worthless if the brain can't act on it — so co-evolve a controller gain θ with h, hoping for a valley neither crosses alone:

\[ w(h,\theta)\;=\;R\,\sigma\!\big(k\,h\,\theta - d\big) - C_h(h) - C_\theta(\theta), \qquad \text{viable}\iff \frac{\partial^2 w}{\partial h\,\partial\theta}\bigg|_{\text{res}} > 0 \]
intuition + measured result

A cheap corner-grid pre-flight measured the cross-partial directly instead of running the full batch. It is not positive: \(\partial^2 B/\partial h\,\partial\theta \approx 0\), the controller pays strongly alone (\(dB/d\theta|_{\text{low }h}=+0.147\) — routing harder escapes the herd with a crude read), and a sharper sensor is pure cost at every θ (\(dB/dh<0\)). The geometry is a θ-ridge with h orthogonally costed — selection drives them apart. No valley to cross.

The maximally-sharp lesson

the generalised L22

A benefit being real, functional-when-gifted, large in bulk (a genuine monomorphic optimum), and demographically affordable (the population survives) is still not sufficient for evolvability. Only the sign of the local gradient at the resident decides — and across seven structurally-distinct levers it stays \(\le 0\).

What would actually be needed (out of this toy's scope): a non-saturating, non-relational, locally-steep affordance — one where a small heritable precision step at the resident already buys a step-change in action value that a crude reader cannot capture. None of the seven toy regimes supplies that, and the obvious "let the controller rescue it" idea was tested and ruled out.

Honest scope — what this is and isn't

What it is

A clean, falsifiable characterization of why a costed sense doesn't evolve in this substrate: the marginal slope, not the installed benefit, governs — measured seven ways, every verdict blinded-verified.

What it isn't

A claim about real biology or larger models. The policy is a provided heuristic, the sensor a noisy-percept stand-in, the costs hand-set; the gifts that were real were tiny; Exp 207 is a design-stage corner-grid, not a full evolved run.

How it was done — and what the method yielded

Two methodological moves did the heavy lifting, and both are now reusable rules:

Reframe the measurement (Exp 203)

Measure the local gradient head-to-head instead of running another evolution. A benefit can be invisible at equilibrium yet still produce a positive relative gradient — only the right assay reveals it.

Pre-flight the premise (Exp 207, L28)

Before a full co-adaptation batch, measure its load-bearing premise (the cross-partial sign) on a ~40-run corner-grid. It falsified the 2-D valley for the cost of a coffee, not a compute-hour.

Other distilled rules from the chapter: L23 (pilot at the full verdict horizon — population metrics are non-monotone), L24 (a healthy-population floor + corr(pop,trait) to reject drift), L25/L26 (a logistic-aware runtime pre-flight; runtime is dominated by population×horizon, not micro-ops), and L27 (multi-agent design-workflow wall-clock is orchestration overhead — pre-digest grounding, tier models, lean schemas — not experiment complexity). The reusable instrument is ecology/sense_axis.py; the gated, byte-identical engine features (enable_residue, enable_niche) carry regression-hash guards so every wall reproduces exactly.
